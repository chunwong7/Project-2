Weather & Traffic Interaction

An exploratory analysis of the effects of weather on traffic accident severity in the United States

Get Started

Data Source

The data set we are using comes from Kaggle. The data contain data from the 48 states on the North American continent plus D.C. collected from Feburary 2016 to December 2019. Next update on the data will come in December 2020.

Source:

  • Moosavi, Sobhan, Mohammad Hossein Samavatian, Srinivasan Parthasarathy, and Rajiv Ramnath. “A Countrywide Traffic Accident Dataset.”, arXiv preprint arXiv:1906.05409 (2019).
  • Moosavi, Sobhan, Mohammad Hossein Samavatian, Srinivasan Parthasarathy, Radu Teodorescu, and Rajiv Ramnath. “Accident Risk Prediction based on Heterogeneous Sparse Data: New Dataset and Insights.” In proceedings of the 27th ACM SIGSPATIAL International Conference on Advances in Geographic Information Systems, ACM, 2019.

Data Preparation

There are 49 columns in the data set. The data that we kept are ID, severity, Start_Time, Start_Lat, Start_Lng, City, State, Temperature(F), Humidity(%), Visibility(mi), Wind_Speed(mph), and Weather_Condition. There are over 100 types of weather condition and we condensed them to 10 unqiue weather conditions. Rows with empty data are discarded. All of the weather data are separated into one CSV while all the accidents data for each state are separated into individual CSVs.
